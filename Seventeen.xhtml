Get busy living or get busy

The initial meaning of life that I have realized as an Afghan is having a peaceful universe. Conflict does not allow youths and teenagers to study securely in their countries. In 2020, when I was in the class, three gunmen stormed the campus of Kabul University, and more than 32 students were killed in this attack. When all students wanted to flee from University, I thought that peace might be the only thing that people need in their society since the violence does not allow youths and teenagers to educate securely; they afraid of terrorist attacks in schools and universities. In addition, for many people who lost their families during wars, peace can be the significant meaning of life for them. In 2019, when I asked one of my friends who lost his mother and sister in a suicide attack, will you forgive terrorists if they join the peace process? He replied, “ However, I lost my family; bringing peace in a conflict society like Afghanistan can save millions of humans in our country, so I will forgive those terrorists who killed my mother and sister.” Therefore, mostly everyone wants peace in a conflict country since they lost their families during decades of wars inside their countries, so they do not want conflicts that kill innocent people. To conclude, being free from disturbance is the first meaning of life for me as an Afghan.
